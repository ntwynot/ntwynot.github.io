  1. What is a 'Wireless Web' site?
    Really, the correct description is an HDML site, for Handheld Device Markup Language, or a WML site for Wireless Markup Language. An HDML or WML site is a specially designed Internet site, usually consisting only of text, that is accessible through the MiniBrowser in your phone.

  2. How many HDML sites are there?
    At the time of the Sprint PCS Wireless Web launch (September, 1999), there are more than 40 HDML sites available.
  3. What kind of sites can I access on my phone?
    An HDML site is a specially designed site, usually consisting only of text, on the Internet that is accessible with the MiniBrowser in your phone.
  4. How do I get to sites not listed in the MiniBrowser menu?
    HDML and WML sites have URLs (Universal Resource Locators, or Internet addresses) just like traditional Internet sites. To access sites not listed on the Wireless Web Browser home page, select Go to..., which appears at the end of the Wireless Web menu. You will then be prompted to enter the URL of an HDML or WML site. The 'http://' is added automatically so you do not need to enter it. The browser will then connect you to the site you have entered. You can also use My Wireless Web to easily create and manage bookmarks for your URLs.
  5. How do I bookmark a site?
    To bookmark a wireless Internet site, you will need the URL (Universal Resource Locator) for that site. Note that the URL for a wireless Internet site may be different than the normal "wired" URL that you use on your computer.
    From your Sprint PCS Phone: To create a bookmark, first Go to the page you want to mark and then access the Browser Menu, which is different from the Wireless Web home page. To learn how to get to the Browser Menu for your specific phone model, check the User Guide for your phone. From the Browser Menu, choose Mark Site. The browser will allow you to edit the name of the bookmark. After you save the bookmark, you will be returned to the page that you just marked.
    From your computer: From the My Sprint PCS Wireless Web main page, select Bookmarks (if Bookmarks does not appear on your list, then click Topics & Layout and add Bookmarks to the list of selected topics). At the Bookmarks page you can create new bookmarks, erase unwanted bookmarks, or edit existing bookmarks.

  6. How do I get to bookmarked sites?
    Once you have bookmarked a site, you can access it by selecting "Bookmarks" from the Sprint PCS home page.
  7. How many bookmarks can I have?
    There is no limit to the number of bookmarks you can create.
  8. Is there a search engine for the Wireless Web Browser or HDML sites?
    Yes. FAST Mobile Search - http://mobile.alltheweb.com.
  9. Do HDML sites have things like 'cookies'?
    Yes, however, cookies are not stored on the phone, but on the Sprint PCS Network server.
  10. Can I buy things with my phone, or use e-commerce sites like Amazon.com?
    Yes. Check out the current list of Sprint PCS Wireless Web sites for more information.
  11. How secure are my Sprint PCS Wireless Web Browser connections?
    Your connections with the Sprint PCS Wireless Web Browser are as secure as your desktop Internet connection. RSA Data Security, whose technologies are part of existing and proposed standards for the Internet, provides security for the MiniBrowser.
  12. Can I design my own HDML site?
    Yes. Please visit developer.sprintpcs.com for information and help with getting started on developing a wireless Internet site.
  13. Can I download things to my phone from the internet?
    Not at this time. Future versions of some Sprint PCS Phones may allow downloads.
  14. How is it priced/billed?
    The Wireless Web Services are billed by the usage time, the same as voice services, or by number of messages/updates in the case of Wireless Web Messaging. You can learn more about applicable rates and service plans for Sprint PCS Wireless Web products and services and our current promotions, by going to "Pricing."
  15. Can I access all the sites on the World Wide Web on my Sprint PCS Phone?
    At the time of the Sprint PCS Wireless Web launch (September, 1999), there were more than 40 HDML sites available.
  16. What happens if I enter an HTML address?
    Although you can enter an HTML address, you will receive an error message on the display screen.
  17. Will I get any images on my phone?
    Most HDML sites are text only, and most phones are only capable of receiving and displaying text. There is nothing in the HDML standard to prevent graphics, however, and future phones will most likely have this capability.
  18. Do I have to sign up for all three Wireless Web services?
     The Wireless Web Option allows you to add Wireless Web services and Wireless Web messages to your existing voice plan. Or you can try the Sprint PCS Wireless Web on a per minute basis.

  19. How fast is the Sprint PCS Wireless Web Browser? What is my connection speed?
    With our current technology, the MiniBrowser connects at 14400bps. Because most pages are text only and limited in size, they load very quickly at this speed.
  20. How does the MiniBrowser compare to the browser in my desktop computer?
    In addition to being smaller, portable, and wireless, the MiniBrowser in your Sprint PCS Phone has a number of differences from your desktop browser. Because most wireless Internet-ready phones have limited memory for caching (storing) recently downloaded information, the MiniBrowser cannot use plug-ins, java-based features, display graphics, or download anything. The sites you can access are different from traditional HTML sites and are designed to be small in size and load quickly so you don't spend time waiting. There are currently no search engines available for HDML web sites.
  21. How does my battery life differ compared to voice calls?
    Battery usage while browsing is virtually the same as during voice calls. Follow the same rules for charging as you would for voice calls.
  22. What if my battery runs out while browsing?
    The call will end. For this reason you should monitor battery strength levels during browser session calls.
  23. Can I receive or place a call while using the browser?
    No. While your Sprint PCS Phone is browsing, all incoming voice calls will be handled as if your phone were turned off. Unlike with voice calls, the Call Waiting feature will not function when your Sprint PCS Phone is in a browser session.
  24. What happens if I receive a call while I am browsing?
    While your phone is browsing, all incoming voice calls will be handled as if your phone were turned off. Unlike with voice calls, the Call Waiting feature will not function when your Sprint PCS Phone is in a browser session.
  25. How strong a signal do I need to use Sprint PCS Wireless Web Connection?
    As long as your phone reports that it is within a Sprint PCS Service Area, you may use Sprint PCS Wireless Web Connection with any level of signal strength. A general rule of thumb is that if you can place a voice call where you are, you can place a browser call, with some exceptions. Note that Wireless Web service may not be immediately available in certain service areas managed by affiliates.
